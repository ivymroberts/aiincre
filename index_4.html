The Premise

The AI in CRE (CREW) working group is a monthly community of practice for CREW members navigating AI adoption in commercial real estate. We meet to share what's working, learn from each other's experience, and ask the harder questions about where this technology is taking our industry and our profession.

We are not here to sell tools or declare winners. We're here to build a practitioner-led knowledge base that's specific to CRE.

"Grounded in real workflows, real concerns, and real results — not vendor pitches or hype cycles."

05 / Tools & Resources

Pick the right tool.
Protect the right data.

A working reference for the AI platforms most likely to show up in CRE workflows — with the questions to ask before you commit. This market moves quickly; verify current terms at vendor sites.

— Before You Commit · Three Questions
01

What's in my input?

If your prompt contains confidential client data, deal terms, financial projections, or personal information — you need enterprise-grade data protection. Consumer-tier tools aren't appropriate for this content.

02

What does my firm allow?

Many firms have IT policies that restrict or prohibit certain tools. Find out before using. If there's no policy, that's a conversation to have with IT — not permission to proceed.

03

What's the task?

Match the tool to the job. Research with citations → Perplexity. Long document analysis → Claude. Excel automation → Copilot. Social content → ChatGPT. Don't default to one tool for everything.

— Platform Reference

ChatGPT

Free · Plus / Team · Enterprise
OpenAI
Best for in CRE

Drafting, research, ideation, summaries. Plus/Team add image generation, web search, custom GPTs, and data analysis.

Watch out for

Free tier trains on your inputs by default — turn off in settings. Team adds privacy controls. Enterprise required for full data isolation.

Claude

Free · Pro · Teams · Enterprise
Anthropic
Best for in CRE

Long documents, contract review, research synthesis, nuanced writing. Cowork (beta) extends to desktop file workflows.

Watch out for

Free has usage limits. Pro ($20/mo) adds larger context and priority access. Teams/Enterprise don't train on your inputs.

Microsoft Copilot

Embedded across M365
Microsoft
Best for in CRE

Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams, PowerPoint — context-aware AI inside the tools you already use every day.

Watch out for

Quality varies significantly by app. Requires M365 Business or Enterprise license. Not a substitute for standalone chat tools.

Gemini

Workspace integration
Google
Best for in CRE

Docs, Sheets, Gmail, real-time web search, Workspace-native context. Strong on data-heavy tasks in Sheets.

Watch out for

Advanced tier needed for full Workspace integration. Less useful if your firm isn't already on Google Workspace.

Perplexity

Research-focused
Perplexity AI
Best for in CRE

Research with live citations, market data, news aggregation, competitive scans. Strong "where did this fact come from" trail.

Watch out for

Not a writing tool — optimized for retrieval, not drafting. Use it for the facts, take them somewhere else for the prose.

Proprietary LLMs

Firm-built or deployed
In-house
Best for in CRE

Maximum data security, firm-specific workflows and institutional knowledge. The endgame for firms with mature AI strategy.

Watch out for

Requires significant technical investment and ongoing maintenance. Not realistic for most smaller CRE firms today.
